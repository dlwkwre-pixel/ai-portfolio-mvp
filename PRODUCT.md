## Register

product

## Users

**Primary:** Serious casual investor — checks in weekly or a few times per week to understand their portfolio, review AI recommendations, and decide whether to buy, sell, trim, hold, or watch. Not a day trader; values clarity and confidence over raw data volume.

**Secondary:** More active investor — checks daily for new ideas, market movement, Reddit/X sentiment, and research opportunities. Wants fast scanning and discovery.

**Research page context:** Users arrive either (a) investigating a specific stock already in mind (search-first) or (b) browsing for new ideas (discovery-first). Both modes must feel native and frictionless.

## Product Purpose

BuyTune.io is an AI-powered portfolio management and stock research co-pilot for self-directed investors who want AI-grade guidance without the complexity of a professional trading terminal. It bridges the gap between passive brokerage apps and overwhelming pro tools — serious enough to be trusted, accessible enough to use in 10 minutes.

Success looks like: a user opening BuyTune, getting a clear picture of their portfolio health, reviewing AI recommendations with enough context to act on them, and discovering 1-2 new stock ideas — all without feeling overwhelmed.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, trustworthy, ambitious.

BuyTune feels like a premium AI investing co-pilot: dark, polished, compact, data-rich, confident, and number-backed. Modern and exciting, but never reckless or gamified. Intelligent first, beautiful second, fast always.

## References

- **Linear** — dark product polish, information density without visual noise, confident typographic hierarchy
- **Perplexity** — AI-assisted research clarity, structured answers that feel trustworthy not flashy
- **TradingView / Koyfin** — market seriousness, data-rich surfaces that professionals respect
- **Robinhood** — ease of use and frictionless action only; not the gamification, not the dopamine loops

## Anti-references

- Meme-stock casino (no rocket emojis, no dopamine loops, no "to the moon" energy)
- Bloomberg Terminal (not cluttered, not intimidating, not raw-data dumps)
- Dated brokerage portals (E*Trade 2015 era — no lifeless grids, no legacy UI patterns)
- Excel / spreadsheet aesthetic (no table-first layouts, no clinical monochrome)
- Dopamine-heavy trading apps (no gamification, no streaks, no push-notification anxiety culture)

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over completeness.** Show the most important signal, not every signal. A user should never have to hunt for the number that matters.
2. **Data earns its place.** Every metric shown must answer a question the user actually has. No vanity stats.
3. **Confidence through precision.** Numbers, labels, and copy should feel exact and authoritative — not hedged, not vague. "Buy 14 / Hold 6 / Sell 2" beats "mostly positive."
4. **Action-oriented surfaces.** Every screen should make the next step obvious: search, research, buy, track, or act on a recommendation.
5. **Compact but breathable.** Information density is a feature, not a problem — but spacing and hierarchy must prevent overwhelm. Dense ≠ cluttered.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum for all text and interactive elements
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — no animation should block task completion
- Sufficient color contrast on all data states (green/red used with labels, not color alone)
- Touch targets minimum 44px on mobile
